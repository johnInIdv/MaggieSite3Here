How College Basketball Gives Us Four
Real Life Davd and Goliath Stories every March.

What is March Madness?

There are 351 NCAA men's basketball teams. Each one of them start the season with an equal chance to play to be the National Champion.

Narrowing the Field

Every March since 1985, 64 teams are chosen from the field to compete in a three week tournament known as March Madness.

The Regions

The 64 teams are split up into four regions.

The Rankings

Within each region, teams are ranked from #1 (the best) to #16 (the worst).

Reality Check

The #1 seeds are considered the four best teams in the country, but often the #16 seeds get into the tournament on a technicality and are not even among the top 100 teams.

And Yet...

they play each other in the opening round of the tournament.

So how do the #16 seeds do?

Match Ups

By far, #16 seeds have played more games against #1 seeds than any other seed. There have been 136 games between the seeds.

This is the Score

This scatterplot shows the scores of all 136 games played between a #1 seed and #16 seed since 1985. The orange dots represent #1 seed victories. The lone red dot is the one victory by a #16 seed.

The Red Dot

On March 15, 2018, after 33 years and 135 consectuvive losses, the unthinkable happened: #16 University of Maryland-Baltimore County (UMBC) beat #1 University of Virginia in Charlotte, NC.

Even More Remarkable

In the previous 135 games, #16 seeds scored just 57 points for every 100 points scored by a #1 seed. In their win, UMBC didn't just win, they won by 20 points.
